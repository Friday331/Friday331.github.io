# Alexander Kilpatrick

Alexander ("Alex") Kilpatrick is an Associate Professor in the [Center for Language Research](https://www.u-aizu.ac.jp/labs/clr/) at the University of Aizu, Japan, and Director of the Language, Cognition, and Computation Laboratory.

His research focuses on computational linguistics, psycholinguistics, information theory, phonology, sound symbolism, machine learning, and language cognition. He is particularly interested in how surprisal, entropy, and phonological structure influence language processing, learning, and memory.

## Research Areas

* Computational linguistics
* Psycholinguistics and language processing
* Information theory in language
* Phonetics and phonology
* Sound symbolism and iconicity
* Statistical modelling of language
* Machine learning and cognitive science

## Current Position

I am an Associate Professor at the University of Aizu, where I conduct research on language and cognition using computational and quantitative methods. My work combines insights from linguistics, psychology, information theory, and machine learning to better understand how humans represent and process language.

## Prospective Students

I welcome enquiries from students interested in graduate supervision. My laboratory works at the intersection of linguistics, cognitive science, and computation, and I am always interested in hearing from motivated students whose research interests align with these areas.

Because I receive a large number of enquiries, I am unable to respond to generic or mass-distributed messages. If you are interested in working with me, please read some of my recent publications and explain specifically how your interests connect to my research. Thoughtful and targeted enquiries will always receive a response.
